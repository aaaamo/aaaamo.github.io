Morpholux + Manta

Date Fall 2016
Type Undergraduate Digital Design Studio
Supervisor Hangman Zho
Collaborators Daye Kim, Kiwon Jeon, Jaejin Lee

MorphoLux is a series of lamp shades created as an introductory project for exploring form-making through Grasshopper. The primary goal was to provide a versatile design mechanism, where users have the freedom to customize the final product to their liking by downloading and modifying an open-source algorithm. By altering the parametric settings—such as the shape, proportions, or tessellation density—users can achieve a wide spectrum of aesthetics, ranging from subtle and minimal to more intricate and sculptural.

Manta was selected in an in-class competition to be installed at the SNU DAAE hall, serving as a pavilion-like spatial intervention. Its initial design began with a single large triangle, which was then broken down into smaller triangles to form a tessellated surface. Anchor points were chosen, and a series of simulated forces pulling towards the ground was applied, allowing the piece to naturally assume its final curved and flowing form. This computational step ensured that the resulting mesh not only looked visually dynamic but also maintained structural logic.
