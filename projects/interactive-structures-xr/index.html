Interactive Structures XR

Date September 2025 - Present
Type Research
Role Research Assistant
Institution Carnegie Mellon University
Lab Regenerative Structures Laboratory

Research assistant at the Regenerative Structures Laboratory, Carnegie Mellon University, supporting development of an XR-enabled design environment through Unity + Meta Quest App for structural form-finding. The project focuses on creating immersive interfaces for design education, allowing students and designers to interact with force-based structural systems in virtual reality. This work explores how extended reality can transform the way architects and engineers understand and prototype structural behavior in real-time.

This product is an exploration of educational software in VR headsets to build, edit and analyze architectural structure systems using graph structure. Users of this app can build simple structures using node and edges augmented in real life.

Toggle node into support nodes or free nodes, then add load at free nodes for analysis. Nodes and edges can be moved in AR space to edit the structure.

Applying Finite Element Method to structure, the forces on each edge members of the structure is visualized in red - tension and blue - compression.
