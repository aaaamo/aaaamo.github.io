Daehong Kim

Architecture & Computational Design

I am Daehong Kim, a computational designer and developer based in architectural design. My work explores architectural modeling representations, generative AI, and the intersection of technology and design, exploring workflows that combine creativity, optimization, and automation. I enjoy experimenting with generative methods to expand the possibilities of architectural form and spatial analysis.

2021 - 2024 Professional Work

Zenerate App

Cloud-based automated feasibility study tool for multifamily modular housing development. Led backend development and core algorithms for a team of 4-5, creating floor plan generation systems that maximize constraints including FAR, density, and height regulations while adhering to building codes.

Generative Plan Design, Optimization Algorithm, Zoning Constraints

Interactive Structures XR

XR-enabled design environment for structural form-finding at Carnegie Mellon University's Regenerative Structures Laboratory. Developing Unity + Meta Quest application for immersive structural prototyping and design education, enabling real-time interaction with force-based form-finding systems in virtual reality.

XR, Structural Design, Unity, Meta Quest, Form-Finding

June 2024 - June 2025 Personal Work

Joint

Experimental web-based service streamlining architectural detailing through automated section detail generation. Uses a skeleton-line approach to instantly create joint details with intuitive editing tools for material priorities and configurations.

Modelling Software, Web Development, BIM
